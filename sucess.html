SYMPTOMS CHECK

Here's a list of possible symptoms and what they might indicate:

Fever:

Elevated body temperature, which can be a sign of infection or inflammation.


Cough:

Persistent coughing may indicate respiratory issues, such as a cold, flu, or respiratory infection.


Shortness of Breath:

Difficulty breathing or breathlessness could be a symptom of various respiratory or cardiac conditions.


Fatigue:

Extreme tiredness or lack of energy, which can be associated with various health issues, including infections, autoimmune disorders, or chronic illnesses.


Headache:

Persistent headaches can be linked to a variety of causes, including stress, tension, migraines, or underlying health conditions.


Muscle or Joint Pain:

Aches in muscles or joints may be related to viral infections, autoimmune disorders, or other inflammatory conditions.


Sore Throat:

Pain or irritation in the throat, often associated with viral or bacterial infections like the common cold or strep throat.


Loss of Taste or Smell:

An abrupt loss of taste or smell could be a symptom of certain viral infections, including COVID-19.


Nausea or Vomiting:

Persistent feelings of nausea or actual vomiting may be indicative of gastrointestinal issues or infections.


Diarrhea:

Frequent loose or watery stools can be a sign of gastrointestinal infections or other digestive disorders.


Remember, this list is not exhaustive, and symptoms can vary based on individual health conditions. It's always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional for a proper diagnosis and guidance.
